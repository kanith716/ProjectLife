Education

We have undertaken various projects educational for underprivileged children with the primary aim of promoting better quality education and holistic development, thereby bridging educational inequity.

A fully paid sponsorship through which employees work as full-time teachers for children from underprivileged backgrounds. Through this direct intervention program we have impacted 400 plus children across schools.

An NGO in West Bengal with our help has built a library and dining space for school children to ensure equitable access to facilities within the school for 500 plus children.

In the east, we are supporting the education of 300 children without parental support.

Expanding the outreach of the education framework, we have provided infrastructural support to 10 schools across North India which cater to the educational needs of differently-abled children.

HDFC Life has also contributed in ensuring ease of transportation for around 200 underprivileged school children in Tamil Nadu, resulting in reduction of expenses incurred by their parents on transportation and thereby removing a barrier to education.

Through one of our projects undertaken in Karnataka, civic literacy and awareness is imparted to children studying in class Ⅷ in 29 lower income schools ensuring not only academic development but long term impact through creating responsible and aware citizens of India.

Empowering children to make informed life and career choices by imparting career and life skills counselling. The children are provided with mentors to guide them in the critical academic years from classes Ⅷ to Ⅹ.

Enabling young girl students in a remote village to attend school regularly by providing cycles to them.

Providing nutritious and healthy mid-day meals to school children.

Health

Health is a crucial area of intervention that aids underprivileged sections of society. Promoting preventive healthcare among them is the first step towards ensuring awareness and improved healthcare.

HDFC Life has tied up for a large project to focus on reducing and preventing protein energy malnutrition among 43,000 children under 5 year, in 24 Gram Panchayats in West Bengal.

Another project has been initiated for children affected with cancer by providing them holistic healthcare and accommodation facilities during the course of treatment. The aim is to reduce the treatment abandonment ratio in underprivileged families.

Providing safe drinking water solutions across six villages and promoting entrepreneurship-based sustainability.

Improving and upgrading sanitation facilities and providing drinking water access to children in 10 municipal schools.

Disabilities Support

There are around 70 million with disabilities in India. Given a fair chance and appropriate opportunities they can contribute to society as well and lead a life of dignity.

Like everyone else, persons with disabilities have abilities, strengths, hopes, dreams and needs. HDFC Life has undertaken a project in Karnataka which involves vocational training to around 200 differently-abled youth and ensuring at least 80 percent placement.

Environmental Sustainability

The impacts of increasing population growth, urbanization and consumption patterns are manifested in the wide degradation of natural resources.

Trees can help solve these problems because of their ability to restore degraded ecosystems, produce food and medicine, and provide environmental and economic benefits.

The importance of planting trees cannot be overstated. At HDFC Life, we have contributed towards environmental sustainability through a large scale tree planting initiative to plant, sustain and grow 4,500 trees in Gurgaon across 3 years.

Philanthropy

HDFC Life helps the underprivileged by providing them sustainable housing solutions. In addition, we have also taken efforts to reach out to the elderly who form an important pillar of society.

We have initiated a rehabilitation project for flood victims in Jammu and Kashmir by supporting the construction of 50 permanent homes.

We have also initiated a project which aims at provides facilities for senior citizens enabling them to live life with ease and dignity. The project helps senior citizens access medical care, household maintenance and social engagement avenues with ease.

In the east region, we are supporting the education of 300 children without parental support.

Employee Volunteerism

Our employees are encouraged to play an active role in contributing to social causes under the Swabhimaan CSR champions program. Employees have spearheaded many activities in their respective branches and locations. This reiterates our values, people engagement, collaboration and integrity thereby creating a stronger sense of social responsibility within the organisation.

Employees play an active role in social causes. Many activities in their respective branches and locations are conceptualised and executed by them.

Over 112 activities have been organised involving more than 1300 employees. These activities include collection drives for books, toys and clothes, educational kits and essential food supplies, tree plantations etc. These drives have been held at regular intervals during the year especially before festivals and during the “Joy of Giving week”. Additionally, blood donation drives were also organised on a regular basis with a tie up with reputed blood banks and hospitals where employees were encouraged to donate blood.

These activities raised awareness about issues faced by the underprivileged sections of the society amongst the employees, and also gave them the avenue to contribute, in a small way to a larger cause

Shaping Careers

“See if your DNA matches ours.”

India opened its insurance industry to the private sector two decades ago and HDFC Life was the first private insurer to get a license. In the years since, a lot has changed – from the way insurance is sold, to what products are sold. However, despite the changes and the resulting growth of the sector, India remains an under-insured country. This potential for further penetration, combined with India’s favorable demographic profile and positive consumption pattern, buttresses the fact that insurance remains one of India’s sunrise sectors.

HDFC Life’s journey over the last two decades has been one of performance-driven and innovation-led growth, with a pioneer’s mindset in many areas including technology, products and distribution.

We believe the key ingredient in our success is our DNA, which is typically expressed in four ways:

First, “We build to last”: HDFC Lifers always think long-term. We are in a 100-year business and our aim is to excel in that kind of long- term sustainability.

Second, “We do chase topline, but not at the cost of profitability”: Like all businesses, we too face topline pressure at different points in a business cycle, but our focus is always on profitable growth. We may invest in a new idea, but we aren't a cash-burning company.

Third, “We compete fiercely, but with prudent risk management”: We believe in sound risk management principles that help prioritize risk over runaway business growth.

And lastly,“We’re paranoid about missing opportunities”: Even at the high points in our journey, we remain anxious about latching on to the next big opportunity before the competition.

If you think that your DNA resonates with what you have heard, then do reach out to us and we would love to have you onboard.

We would like to bring to the notice of all concerned that we i.e. HDFC Life Insurance Company Limited follow meritocracy and have talent-based employee recruitment processes.We do not charge/ accept any amount or security deposit from job seekers while inviting candidates for an interview or during any stage of the selection process.

It has come to our notice that some unscrupulous persons are posing themselves as employees/representatives/agents of HDFC Life with an ulterior motive to make money through unfair means from the prospective job seekers. They are fraudulently offering jobs in HDFC Life through some websites or telephone calls or fake letters and are asking the job seekers to deposit certain amounts in bank accounts.

The public in general is hereby advised not to fall prey to such fraudulent activities. HDFC Life does not ask, solicit or accept any monies in any form from the job applicants, whether online or otherwise as a pre-employment requirement. We also do not authorise any vendor to collect money on our behalf, for employment with HDFC Life. HDFC Life bears no responsibility for amounts being deposited / withdrawn there from in response to such offers.

As an alert jobseeker, please look out for the following pointers (illustrative only and not exhaustive) to identify fraudulent job offers:

  • A fraudulent job offer will ask for money to be deposited in the bank account of an individual or to be paid in cash. As stated above HDFC Life does not ask for money to be paid in lieu of employment nor has it authorized any of its employees to ask for money for the same.
  • A job offer letter will be emailed from an internet email id viz.gmail, hotmail,rediffmail,yahoo,etc. Please note HDFC Life’s correspondence is only through the official domain emailids and no correspondence related to employment or recruitment will ever be done from any other domain.

Anyone dealing with such fake interview communications or job offers would be doing so at his / her own risk and cost and HDFC Life will not be held responsible/ liable for any loss or damage suffered by such persons, directly or indirectly.

Further please note that at HDFC Life, we offer employment to students who have successfully cleared specific insurance courses offered by our partners: Manipal Global Education Services Private Limited(hereinafter referred as Manipal) and upGrad Education Private Limited(hereinafter referred as upGrad) subject to meeting certain conditions. Manipal and upGrad charge course fees to students for the educational training and all correspondence related to payment of course fees will come from Manipal and upGrad domains only. No payment is solicited by or should be paid to HDFC Life or to persons/ vendors representing HDFC Life for these programs either online or by cash.

Should you require any further information about genuineness or otherwise of such communication which you may receive, you are requested to get in touch with the Human Resources team on the following email id – hrops@hdfclife.com during business hours.
